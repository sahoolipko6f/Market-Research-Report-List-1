<p><p>The photodegradable plastic market primarily consists of two types: copolymers and additives. Copolymers are specialized polymers designed to break down under sunlight exposure, incorporating light-sensitive chemical structures into their makeup. In contrast, additives are compounds added to traditional plastics to enhance their degradation properties when exposed to UV light. Both types aim to mitigate environmental impact by facilitating faster degradation compared to conventional plastics, catering to growing consumer demand for sustainable packaging solutions.</p></p>
