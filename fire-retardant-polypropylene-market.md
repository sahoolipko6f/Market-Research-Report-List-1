<p><p>The Fire Retardant Polypropylene market is divided into two main types: Halogen Type and Halogen-Free Type. Halogen Type fire retardants contain bromine or chlorine, effectively reducing flammability but raising concerns about toxic emissions during combustion. In contrast, Halogen-Free Type fire retardants utilize alternative materials, such as phosphorous or aluminum trihydrate, to enhance fire resistance without releasing harmful gases, thus appealing to environmentally conscious consumers. Both types serve crucial roles in applications like automotive, construction, and electronics.</p></p>
